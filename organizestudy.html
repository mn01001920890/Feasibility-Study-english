Executive Summary

This study aims to provide a comprehensive analysis of the legal and regulatory aspects of a car protection and nano ceramic center project in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. This project is considered modern and advanced in the automotive services sector, offering specialized services in applying Paint Protection Film (PPF), nano ceramic coatings, window tinting, and car polishing.

The study includes a detailed analysis of legal and regulatory requirements, necessary licenses, compliance with local laws and regulations, legal risk management, the required administrative structure, tax and Zakat obligations, intellectual property rights, required insurance, and occupational safety and health requirements.

Study Objectives

  • Identify all legal and regulatory requirements for the project.

  • Analyze legal risks and develop strategies to mitigate them.

  • Provide practical recommendations for managing legal and regulatory aspects.

Legal and Regulatory Framework

The car protection and nano ceramic center project is subject to a comprehensive set of laws and regulations in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, which aim to regulate commercial activities, ensure public safety, and protect the rights of all involved parties.

Governing Primary Regulations

Saudi Companies Law

The Companies Law issued by Royal Decree No. M/132 dated 1/12/1443H is the primary legal framework for establishing and operating companies in the Kingdom. This law defines the different forms of companies and the requirements for incorporation, registration, and governance.

Available Company Forms for the Project:

  • Sole Proprietorship: Simpler procedures, suitable for small projects.
  • Limited Liability Company (LLC): Greater protection for owners, broader growth potential.

Saudi Labor Law

The Labor Law issued by Royal Decree No. M/51 dated 23/8/1426H governs labor relations in the Kingdom. This law specifies the rights and duties of employers and workers, terms of employment contracts, working hours, wages, leaves, termination of service, and occupational safety and health requirements.

Municipal and Urban Planning Regulations

Automotive repair and service workshops are subject to specific municipal requirements according to regulations issued by the Ministry of Municipal and Rural Affairs and Housing. These requirements include location, area, design, equipment, safety, and environmental standards.

Requirement Details Competent Authority

Specialized Regulatory Laws

Consumer Protection Laws

The Consumer Protection Law issued by Royal Decree No. M/7 dated 11/2/1429H governs consumer rights and the obligations of service providers. This law requires the car protection center to adhere to quality and transparency standards in dealing with customers.

Required Licenses and Permits

Establishing and operating a car protection and nano ceramic center requires obtaining a comprehensive set of licenses and permits from the relevant government authorities.

Primary Licenses

Commercial Registration (CR)

The Commercial Registration is the first and most important legal step to officially establish the project. The CR is obtained through the Ministry of Commerce's electronic platform (cr.sa).

Required Documents:

  • National ID of the project owner
  • National Address for the designated business location
  • Mobile number activated with Absher
  • Specify business activity: "Automotive Services - Vehicle Tinting and Protection"

Fees: 200 SAR + Chamber of Commerce fees 300-800 SAR

Issuance Period: 1-3 business days

Municipal License

The municipal license is necessary to operate the shop legally and connect utilities. It is obtained through the Balady platform (balady.gov.sa).

Safety and Security Licenses

Civil Defense Safety Certificate

A safety certificate is mandatory for workshops containing heating devices or flammable materials.

Basic Requirements:

  • Fire extinguishers (one for every 50 sq. meters)
  • Clear and illuminated emergency exits
  • Smoke and heat detectors
  • Equipped first aid kit
  • Warning and instructional signs
  • Adequate ventilation for chemical materials

Regulatory Compliance

Regulatory compliance is the cornerstone of ensuring the legal and safe operation of the car protection and nano ceramic center. This compliance requires a deep understanding of local laws, regulations, and industry standards, and the implementation of strict procedures to ensure ongoing adherence to all regulatory requirements.

Permit and License Requirements

Continuous Operational Licenses

The continuous operation of the center requires maintaining the validity of all required licenses and permits. This includes the periodic renewal of the Commercial Registration, Municipal License, and Safety Certificate, and complying with any updates or amendments to regulatory requirements.

The renewal process requires a periodic review of compliance with the original conditions and may include field inspections from competent authorities to ensure requirements are still being met. A pre-reminder system for renewal dates should be established to avoid license interruptions, which could lead to work stoppages or fines.

Environmental and Protection Regulations

The center is subject to strict environmental regulations due to the use of chemical materials in cleaning and protection processes. These requirements include obtaining permits to handle hazardous materials, implementing safe storage procedures, and ensuring the proper disposal of chemical waste.

Waste Management Requirements:

  • Separate chemical waste from regular waste.
  • Contract with certified specialized companies for hazardous waste disposal.
  • Document all disposal processes for review by environmental authorities.
  • Develop a comprehensive waste management plan.

Industry and Quality Standards

International Standards for Protection and Application

Applying protection films and nano-ceramic coatings requires adherence to strict international quality and performance standards. These standards include using genuine, manufacturer-approved materials, applying certified installation techniques, and ensuring the final quality of the work.

Accreditation and Training Certificates

Maintaining high-quality standards requires continuous training for technicians and updating their certifications. Required training programs include specialized courses in applying protection films, nano-ceramic techniques, advanced cleaning methods, and safety procedures.

Compliance Monitoring and Continuous Assessment

Internal Monitoring Systems

Compliance Officer Responsibilities:

  • Monitor the implementation of all regulatory requirements.

Regulatory compliance requires maintaining detailed records and submitting periodic reports to the competent authorities. These reports include data on chemical use, quantities of waste generated, safety measures implemented, and work accidents, if any.

Intellectual Property Rights

Intellectual property rights are a vital aspect of the car protection and nano ceramic center's operations, as the project deals with advanced technologies and famous global brands. This requires a deep understanding of intellectual property laws in Saudi Arabia and strict adherence to protecting the rights of others and the project's own rights.

Trademark Protection

Trademarks for Materials and Products

The center uses products from famous global brands such as XPEL, 3M, GYEON, and LLumar, all of which are protected by international and local intellectual property laws. Using these products requires obtaining official licenses from the manufacturers or their authorized distributors in the Kingdom.

Requirements for Legal Use:

  • Ensure all materials used are genuine and imported through official channels.
  • Retain all documents proving the legitimacy of product acquisition.
  • Purchase invoices, certificates of authenticity, and distribution licenses.
  • Adhere to the marketing guidelines issued by each manufacturer.

Use of Trademarks in Marketing

The center has the right to use the trademarks of the products it uses in marketing and advertising, but within specific limits and under certain conditions. It must adhere to the marketing guidelines issued by each manufacturer, which specify how to use the trademark, logos, and corporate colors.

Protection of Technologies and Processes

Specialized Application Technologies

The processes of applying protection films and nano-ceramic coatings involve specialized techniques that may be protected by patents or trade secrets. It must be ensured that all technologies used are legally licensed and that technicians are trained and certified by the manufacturers.

Required Protection Measures:

  • Sign Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) with all employees.
  • Establish strict procedures to protect information from leakage.
  • Train employees on the legal use of technologies.
  • Avoid any copyright infringements.

Design and Cutting Software

Some types of protection films use specialized design software, such as XPEL's DAP program, to design custom cuts for each car model. These programs are protected by copyright and their use requires official licenses.

Developing the Center's Brand Identity

Registering the Center's Trademark

It is highly recommended to register a distinctive trademark for the center with the Saudi Authority for Intellectual Property (SAIP). This registration provides legal protection for the trade name and logo and prevents others from using them without permission.

Tax and Zakat Obligations

Tax and Zakat obligations are an important aspect of managing the car protection and nano ceramic center, requiring compliance with all financial and tax requirements in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. This demands a deep understanding of tax and Zakat systems and the implementation of precise procedures to ensure full compliance.

Value Added Tax (VAT) System

VAT Registration

Saudi law requires establishments with annual revenues exceeding 375,000 SAR to register for VAT. For the car protection center, it is expected to exceed this threshold within the first year of operation, making registration mandatory.

Registration Procedures:

  1. Submit the registration application via the FATOORA e-invoicing platform.
  2. Provide required documents (Commercial Registration, Municipal License, financial statements).
  3. Wait for approval and receive the tax number.
  4. Apply VAT to all services.

Expected Duration: 15-30 business days

Fees: Free

Applying VAT to Services

All car protection services are subject to a 15% VAT:

Services Subject to Tax:

  • Paint Protection Film (PPF) Application
  • Nano Ceramic Application
  • Window Tinting
  • Polishing and Cleaning Services
  • Sale of Products and Accessories

Zakat System

Zakat Registration

Saudi establishments are subject to the Zakat system according to Islamic Sharia provisions and local laws. Registration in the Zakat system is required within 60 days of starting the business.

Calculating the Zakat Base

The Zakat base is calculated on the net Zakat-able assets of the establishment:

Item Details Treatment
Cash and Deposits Cash on hand and bank accounts Added to base
Inventory Stored materials and products Added to base
Accounts Receivable Amounts due from customers Added to base
Accounts Payable Amounts due to suppliers Deducted from base

Zakat Rate: 2.5% of net Zakat-able assets

Expected Tax Costs

Labor Licenses and Labor Law

Compliance with the Saudi Labor Law and obtaining the appropriate labor licenses are among the most important legal requirements for operating the car protection and nano ceramic center. This requires a comprehensive understanding of labor laws, Saudization requirements, and recruitment procedures for local and expatriate labor.

Saudization Requirements

Required Saudization Ratios

According to the Nitaqat program, establishments in the car services sector must achieve specific Saudization ratios to obtain the appropriate classification. For small establishments (fewer than 10 employees):

Green Category (Required):

  • Saudization Ratio: At least 20%
  • For a facility with 5 employees: at least one Saudi employee
  • For a facility with 8 employees: at least two Saudi employees

Benefits of the Green Category:

  • Ability to recruit new expatriate labor.
  • Renewal of work visas for current labor.
  • Access to services from the Ministry of Human Resources.
  • Discounts on expatriate labor fees.

Expatriate Labor Licenses

Recruitment Procedures

Basic Requirements:

  • Achieve Green category in Nitaqat.
  • Have sufficient balance on the Qiwa platform.
  • Meet Saudization requirements.
  • Pay expatriate labor fees.

Recruitment Steps:

  1. Submit a recruitment request via the Qiwa platform.
  2. Specify the required profession and nationality.
  3. Pay the required fees (2,000 SAR per worker).
  4. Wait for ministry approval.
  5. Issue the work visa.
  6. Receive the worker and complete procedures.
